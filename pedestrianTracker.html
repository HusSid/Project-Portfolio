Pedestrian & Car Detection

To track pedestrians and cars, certain visual features can be identified which distinguish them from their surroundings. Using a pre-trained classifier for pedestrians and cars, they can both be detected in real-time from a video feed.

The algorithm could successfully highlight pedestrians in red and cars in green but its accuracy was limited by:
  • The classifier, which struggles to identify every subject due to inherent limitations of the model.
  • Visual limitations such as lighting and image resolution.

Face Detection

Similarly, using another pre-trained classifier, faces can be detected from both images and videos. As shown below, the classifier is successful in identifying individual faces and in clear group photos.

The classifier does struggle, however, in group photos where the faces are not directly facing the camera and can miss some faces.

Model accuracy can be improved to a certain extent by using images of faces in different positions, lighting as well as partial images during training so that the classifier can still recognise the relevant features of a face even when the face is not explicitly visible.

Smile Detection

Another pre-trained classifier was used to detect if someone was smiling in addition to the face detection methods shown previously.

In many cases, this method was able to identify faces and recognise if that face is smiling but it also suffers from similar limitations as the other classifiers which reduces its accuracy.

Furthermore, the classifier searched for smiles within the bounding box of the face which means that the accuracy of the face detection classifier was another limiting factor. If, instead, the classifier explored the entire image for a smile, more smiles might be detected but it could also lead to results like the image below.

Training the neural network with lots of different types of smiles under various visibility conditions could help the classifier better distinguish between a person smiling and other similar shapes up to a certain level of precision, beyond which the accuracy of the classification method itself becomes a limiting factor.

Face Recognition

A face recognition API was used to recognise individual faces. This method uses a deep convolutional neural network to generate measurements that describe a person's face which can then be used to differentiate between different people. Using a reference image for a person, subsequent images can be compared and assigned a score based on how closely the two images match.
