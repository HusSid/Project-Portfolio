Structured Light Scanner

A scanner was designed and built to create CAD models of engine components for Imperial College London's Dynamics Group, consisting of a turntable to rotate the objects 360Â° and a projector and camera for scanning.

Structured light was the chosen scanning method, which projects a series of patterns onto the object and by measuring the depth profile, generates point cloud data for the 3D mesh.

Initial models were of low quality so to improve, a higher resolution projector and camera were used to acheive higher density point clouds.

Scanning shiny objects was difficult due to surface reflections so a possible solution was to coat the surface for a matte finish, resulting in a higher density point cloud and better 3D model.

The final design could not be tested due to Covid-19 but evaluation of the prototype showed that 3D models could be accurate to within 0.5mm.
