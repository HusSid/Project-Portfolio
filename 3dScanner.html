Structured Light Scanner

A scanner was designed and built to create high resolution CAD models of engine components for Imperial College London's Dynamics Group with minimal user input, consisting of a turntable to rotate the objects 360Â° and a projector and camera for scanning.

Structured light was the chosen scanning method, which projects a series of patterns onto the object and by measuring the depth profile, generates point cloud data for the 3D mesh.

Initial models were of low quality so to improve, a higher resolution projector and camera were used to acheive higher density point clouds and better 3D models.

Scanning shiny objects was difficult due to surface reflections so a possible solution was to coat the surface for a matte finish, resulting in a higher density point cloud and increasing the resolution of the 3D model.

The final design was built and programmed but, unfortunately, could not be tested due to the COVID-19 pandemic. Evaluation of the prototype, however, showed that 3D models could be accurate to within 0.5mm hence the final design would have likely met the accuracy requirements set out in the design specification.
